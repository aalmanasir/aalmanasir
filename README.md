## Profile

I am an Emirati author, arbitration researcher, and public-sector professional based in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.

My work follows two interconnected paths: **legal scholarship** and **literary authorship**.

Through legal research, I examine international commercial arbitration, contractual interpretation, legal legitimacy, institutional authority, and the implications of algorithmic transformation for justice and human decision-making.

Through literature, I explore memory, identity, absence, belonging, love, time, emotional language, and the invisible connections that endure between people.

My professional experience also spans healthcare support operations, parking and transportation, protective services, executive protocol, stakeholder coordination, accessibility, and patient and visitor experience.

## Volume I — Three Months, Four Seasons

### ثلاثة أشهر، أربعة فصول

A meditation on time, companionship, memory, and the emotional seasons through which human relationships pass.

The volume considers how a brief period may contain the emotional weight of an entire year—and how certain people remain present long after their season has ended.

---

## Volume II — The Fifth Season

### الفصل الخامس

A philosophical exploration of what remains after the recognised seasons of a relationship have passed.

The fifth season is not a season on the calendar. It is the emotional space inhabited by memory, absence, unfinished meaning, and the people who continue to live within us after departure.

---

## Volume III — How Is Life Treating You?

### كيف تُعامِلُكَ الحياة؟

A reflective dialogue with life itself.

The volume explores human endurance, emotional fatigue, ordinary struggle, hope, identity, and the private questions concealed beneath a simple social expression:

> How is life treating you?

---

## Volume IV — Divorce Me… Then Marry Me

### طَلِّقْنِي… ثُمَّ تَزَوَّجْنِي

A poetic and philosophical meditation on marriage, separation, identity, freedom, emotional language, and the possibility of choosing love again.

The work does not portray either partner as entirely right or wrong. It considers how two sincere people may love one another deeply while failing to understand the language through which the other needs to receive that love.

Its central proposition is:

> Love does not say, “Remain because you are mine.”  
> It says, “Become fully yourself; and if you still choose me, come.”

The manuscript is currently in development in Arabic and English.

---

## Literary Philosophy

My literary work returns to several recurring ideas:

- Memory does not merely preserve the past; it reshapes identity.
- Absence may remain emotionally present.
- Human connection may endure beyond time, distance, and separation.
- Love without freedom risks becoming possession.
- Silence may protect a relationship temporarily while destroying it gradually.
- People may be sincere and still misunderstand one another completely.
- Emotional truth is rarely divided cleanly between victim and offender.
- The deepest transformations often occur between departure and return.
- Not every separation is an ending.
- Not every return is repetition.
- The people we lose may continue to influence who we become.
- Ordinary moments can carry extraordinary emotional meaning.

## Volume II — The Fifth Season

### الفصل الخامس

A philosophical exploration of what remains after the recognised seasons of a relationship have passed.

The fifth season is not a season on the calendar. It is the emotional space inhabited by memory, absence, unfinished meaning, and the people who continue to live within us after departure.

## Volume III — How Is Life Treating You?

### كيف تُعامِلُكَ الحياة؟

A reflective dialogue with life itself.

The volume explores human endurance, emotional fatigue, ordinary struggle, hope, identity, and the private questions concealed beneath a simple social expression:

> How is life treating you?

---

## Volume IV — Divorce Me… Then Marry Me

### طَلِّقْنِي… ثُمَّ تَزَوَّجْنِي

A poetic and philosophical meditation on marriage, separation, identity, freedom, emotional language, and the possibility of choosing love again.

The work does not portray either partner as entirely right or wrong. It considers how two sincere people may love one another deeply while failing to understand the language through which the other needs to receive that love.

Its central proposition is:

> Love does not say, “Remain because you are mine.”  
> It says, “Become fully yourself; and if you still choose me, come.”

The manuscript is currently in development in Arabic and English.

## Literary Philosophy

My literary work returns to several recurring ideas:

- Memory does not merely preserve the past; it reshapes identity.
- Absence may remain emotionally present.
- Human connection may endure beyond time, distance, and separation.
- Love without freedom risks becoming possession.
- Silence may protect a relationship temporarily while destroying it gradually.
- People may be sincere and still misunderstand one another completely.
- Emotional truth is rarely divided cleanly between victim and offender.
- The deepest transformations often occur between departure and return.
- Not every separation is an ending.
- Not every return is repetition.
- The people we lose may continue to influence who we become.
- Ordinary moments can carry extraordinary emotional meaning.
